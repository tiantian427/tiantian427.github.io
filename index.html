About

Research profile

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Finance at Guanghua School of Management, Peking University. My primary fields are political economy, economic history, and public economics.

Across projects, I ask a common question: when formal structures of authority move, what actually changes on the ground? Empirically, I work with large-scale administrative data, judicial case records, regulatory texts, and financial market data.

Research

Working papers and work in progress

My current projects study how institutional reforms reshape screening, financing, adjudication, and risk transmission under imperfect state capacity.

01
With Prof. Li-An Zhou · Draft available

Who Screens for the State? Recentralization and Local Control in Environmental Review

This paper examines a recentralization reform where environmental review authority shifted from county to city governments. We find that instead of simply strengthening capacity, the reform reorganized local protectionism and created selective intermediary chains that retain low-visibility compliance problems.

02
Reject and Resubmit at Management Science

Securing the Care Workforce: How Public Insurance Commitments Reshape Human Capital Pipelines

Uses China’s Long-Term Care Insurance pilot and Gaokao admission data to study how public commitment reshapes healthcare human capital pipelines.

03
Sole Author · Draft available · 2026 ARIA Annual Meeting

The Shadow Fiscal Financier: Insurance Capital, Regulatory Shocks, and Local Government Debt in China

Identifies insurance capital as a shadow financier for local government debt. Using a granular Bartik IV based on liability-side regulatory shocks, I show that insurer yield hunger compresses LGFV credit spreads.

04
with T. Hao & C. Wu · Major Revision at Journal of Risk and Insurance

Accelerating Fragility: Climate Shocks, Information Contagion, and Systemic Risk in Opaque Insurance Markets

Studies how climate shocks and information contagion amplify systemic risk in opaque insurance markets.
